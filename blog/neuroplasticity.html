Neuroplasticity

Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity describes the way the nervous system but particularly the brain can adapt, learn and recover from injury. As it pertains to leaning, neuroplasticity describes how experiences reorganise and build neural pathways in the brain. Just understanding the principles of neuroplasticity can be beneficial in many aspects of a persons life by allowing you to engage with the learning process in a more meaningful way, building and strengthening neural pathways ways that improve your knowledge, skills and relationships.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

One of the more important ways you can improve your neuroplasticity is by cultivating a growth mindset. The way you approach the learning process effects the way you learn and a growth mindset enhances growth and strengthening of neural pathways. Simply understanding that the brain remains adaptable past childhood and recontextualizing challenging material not as proof of inadequacy, but instead opportunities for growth can have a positive effect on your ability to overcome learning challenges.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Some ways to improve your neuroplasticity are…

  • Repetition of material: Strengthen and link exiting neural pathways.
  • Specificity: Focusing on the right material for best effect.
  • Intensity: Higher intensity of learning allows for increased growth.
  • Salience: The training has to be meaningful for best effect.
  • Transference: Change in function as a result of a training experience can lead to learning other similar skills.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Growth mindset is trust in the idea that you can learn and adapt overtime. That struggling with a problem isn’t proof of inadequacy, but the process of improving your mind. A growth mindset helps prime your brain to better engage with challenges of the classroom, where a fixed mindset can lock down when it encounters a blocker.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I was already familiar with the concepts surrounding neuroplasticity but the idea of a growth mindset alone is a powerful tool, especially considering the difficulty of the challenges we will expect to face during Bootcamp.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will incorporate it into my reflections at the end of exercises, making sure that I approached the challenge with a growth mindset in every facet.
